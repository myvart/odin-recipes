Pottage of Peas and Bacon

This roast capon, flavored with a blend of ginger, cloves, and black pepper, reflects the medieval love of spiced meats. The combination of vinegar and honey forms a rich glaze that caramelizes as the bird roasts, creating a balance of sweet, savory, and tangy flavors. Capon was a prized bird, often served at feasts and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried peas
  • 4 strips of bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Soak the dried peas overnight, then drain.
  2. In a pot, fry the bacon until crispy. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, saut√© the chopped onion in the bacon fat until soft.
  4. Add the peas, thyme, bay leaf, and enough water to cover the peas. Simmer for 1 to 2 hours until peas are tender.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, stir in the bacon, and serve.
